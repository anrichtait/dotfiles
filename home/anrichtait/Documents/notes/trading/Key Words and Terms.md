1. **GDP (Gross Domestic Product):**
   - The total value of goods and services produced within a country in a specific period. It's a key indicator of economic health.

2. **Retail Sales:**
   - A measure of the total sales of goods by retailers. It’s an important indicator of consumer spending and economic activity.

3. **BOE (Bank of England):**
   - The central bank of the United Kingdom, responsible for setting interest rates and managing monetary policy.

4. **Quarter-point Reductions:**
   - A decrease in interest rates by 0.25%. Central banks adjust rates to control inflation and stimulate or cool down the economy.

5. **Inflation:**
   - The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, eroding purchasing power.

6. **Federal Reserve (Fed):**
   - The central banking system of the United States, which regulates the U.S. monetary and financial system.

7. **Basis Points:**
   - A unit of measure used in finance to describe the percentage change in interest rates. One basis point is equal to 0.01%.

8. **Easing:**
   - A monetary policy stance where central banks reduce interest rates or take other actions to stimulate the economy.

9. **Initial Jobless Claims:**
   - A measure of the number of individuals who file for unemployment benefits for the first time. It's a leading indicator of labor market conditions.

10. **Manufacturing Data:**
    - Economic data that tracks the production output of the manufacturing sector, indicating the health of the industry.

11. **AI (Artificial Intelligence):**
    - The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems, such as learning, reasoning, and self-correction.

12. **Supercore Inflation:**
    - A measure of inflation that excludes the most volatile components, such as food and energy, to give a clearer picture of underlying inflation trends.

13. **PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures):**
    - A measure of price changes in consumer goods and services, reflecting inflation as experienced by consumers.

14. **PPI (Producer Price Index):**
    - An index that measures the average change in selling prices received by domestic producers for their output.

15. **Jackson Hole Economic Policy Symposium:**
    - An annual conference held in Jackson Hole, Wyoming, where central bankers, economists, and financial market participants discuss economic issues.

16. **CPI (Consumer Price Index):**
    - A measure that examines the weighted average of prices of a basket of consumer goods and services, such as transportation, food, and medical care.

17. **Two-year Treasury Yields:**
    - The return on investment for U.S. government debt securities with a two-year maturity. It reflects market expectations of future interest rates.

18. **Labor Market:**
    - The supply and demand for labor, where workers find paying work, employers find willing workers, and wage levels are determined.
19. __Public Company__:
    - Companies whose stocks are listed on a stock exchange and whose shares can be bought or sold
20. **Mergers**:
    - The combination of two companies into a single entity, where both companies mutually agree to join together as equals.
21. **Takeovers**:
    - The acquisition of one company by another, often through the purchase of a controlling interest in the target company’s shares, which may be friendly or hostile.
22. **Acquisitions**:
    - The process by which one company purchases another company, either entirely or by buying a majority stake, to expand its operations or gain new assets.
