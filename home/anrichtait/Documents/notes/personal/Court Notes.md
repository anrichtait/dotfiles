## Prep for case:
### **1. Documentation of Non-Compliance:**
   - **Court Orders:** Copies of all court orders related to visitation, therapy, and financial obligations.
   - **Proof of Non-Compliance:** Evidence showing his failure to comply with these orders, such as missed therapy payments, missed visits, or failure to follow court-mandated conditions.

### **2. Evidence of Harmful Behaviour:**
   - **Incident Reports:** Documentation of any incidents involving the father, such as the incident where he fired rounds into the air. Include police reports, restraining orders, or other official records.
   - **Substance Abuse Records:** Documentation or evidence of his substance abuse issues, including any failed drug tests or rehab attempts.
   - **Communication Records:** Copies of communications from the father that demonstrate harassment, threats, or other problematic behaviour (e.g., text messages, emails, voicemails).

### **3. Psycho-social and Professional Reports:**
   - **Psychosocial Reports:** Existing psychosocial reports that detail the impact of the father’s behavior on the children.
   - **Therapist or Counselor Reports:** Reports from therapists or counselors who have worked with the children, describing the emotional and psychological impact of the father’s actions and their recommendations.

### **4. Evidence of Emotional and Psychological Impact on Children:**
   - **Testimonies from Therapists:** Statements or affidavits from mental health professionals who can attest to the children’s emotional state and the effect of the father’s behavior.
   - **Children’s Statements:** If appropriate and considering their age, written or verbal statements from the children expressing their feelings about their father’s behavior and their wishes regarding contact.

### **5. Financial Documentation:**
   - **Proof of Financial Neglect:** Documentation showing the father’s failure to provide financial support for the children, including unpaid child support or therapy costs.

### **6. Legal and Procedural Documents:**
   - **Previous Court Filings:** Copies of previous legal filings related to custody, visitation, and support.
   - **Motion to Terminate Parental Rights:** Draft or outline of the motion you plan to file, including the legal grounds for the request and the supporting evidence.

### **7. General Case Information:**
   - **Family History:** A summary of the family’s history with the father, including any previous court cases or interventions.
   - **Detailed Timeline:** A chronological timeline of events and incidents involving the father, including court dates, missed obligations, and significant events impacting the children.

### **8. Witness Statements:**
   - **Affidavits from Witnesses:** Statements from individuals who have witnessed the father’s behavior or the impact of his actions on the children (e.g., teachers, family members).

### **Steps for Gathering Information:**

1. **Organize Documents:** Create a systematic and organized file for each type of evidence. Label and categorize documents clearly to ensure easy retrieval.
2. **Consult Professionals:** Reach out to legal professionals, social workers, or counselors who can assist with gathering and interpreting relevant information.
3. **Request Records:** Obtain official records from relevant authorities, such as police reports or court records.
4. **Secure Statements:** Ask professionals who have worked with the children to provide detailed affidavits or statements regarding their observations and recommendations.
5. **Update Records Regularly:** Continuously update your records with any new incidents or developments related to the case.

### **Working with Legal Professionals:**
   - **Family Lawyer:** Consult with a family lawyer who can help you understand what evidence is most compelling, assist in drafting the motion, and guide you through the legal process.
   - **Legal Aid:** If financial constraints are an issue, consider seeking help from legal aid organizations that provide support for family law matters.
